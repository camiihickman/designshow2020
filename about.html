Judges

Borzou Azabdaftari

Owner of Falcon Lab

Borzou is the owner of The Falcon Lab, a creative, digital and print agency in Tyson’s Corner, VA. They focus on not having a focus and doing a wide variety of projects for Fortune 500 companies down to SMBs. When he’s not working he’s either spending time with his family, traveling or trying to make the world a better place. Ideally all three at once.

Cara Carpizzi

Cara creates smart and approachable design solutions to help build brands and support marketing needs. Cara delivers solutions that create an impact, engage the audience and help build the business. Cara loves to build long-lasting relationships with her clients and work as an extension of the clients’ team or work on a per-project basis in areas including but not limited to event promotion and on-site materials, logo and brand development, annual reports and publications, and infographics, etc.

Luke Miller

UX Designer at National Geographic

Luke is currently the UX Director for National Geographic Society and has been working on digital media products for 10 years. He also teaches at AU and George Washington in the fall. Prior to life in DC Luke worked and taught in Singapore and New York City.

Phim Her

Marketing Solutions Manager at The Washington Post

Phim is a marketing solutions manager at The Washington Post, where she leverages her strategic marketing and creative storytelling expertise to support democracy and the free press. She is an active member of AIGA, where she most recently served as Co-Chair with Douglas Davis on the National Diversity & Inclusion Task Force and Content Lead on the National EMERGE Initiative.

Raksa Yin

Product Designer at Deloitte Digital

Raksa Yin is a Product Designer at Deloitte Digital, where he is creating digital experiences for the government sector. His former agencies includes Huge Inc, Grafik, and Insomniac Design. He also serves on AIGA’s local and national committees empowering the creative community. He comes from humble beginnings as a son of immigrant refugees. This inspires him to use creativity to improve the world. He received his Bachelor in Graphic Design from Corcoran School of Art and Design at GWU.
